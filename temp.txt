Here are the most logical next steps, categorized by importance:

1. The Final MVP Piece (Highly Recommended)
The Leaderboard / Public Results: The judges are scoring projects, but right now, those scores just sit on the Judge's dashboard. You need a view where Participants and Organizers can actually see the final standings and find out who won the prize money!

2. Polishing the Core Experience (Good to Have)
Event Status Management: Right now, your hackathons are probably just "open" forever. Organizers need a way to change the status of an event (e.g., Draft → Registration Open → Hacking Active → Judging → Completed).

User Profiles: Allowing users to add their actual names, GitHub usernames, LinkedIn profiles, and maybe a profile picture, rather than just seeing participant@gmail.com everywhere.

Specific Judge Assignment: Right now, it looks like any logged-in Judge sees all submissions across the whole platform. You probably want a way for Organizers to invite specific judges to their specific hackathons.

3. "Version 2.0" Features (Advanced)
Announcements System: An Organizer panel where they can blast out a message (like "Pizza is here!" or "1 hour remaining!") that appears on the Participant dashboards.

Search and Filter: Adding a search bar and category tags to the "Find Hackathons" page so users can filter by "Upcoming", "Active", or "Past".
